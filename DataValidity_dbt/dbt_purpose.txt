dbt is useful as an **analytics engineering / data transformation** tool with some strong data validity features.

### Where dbt *does* act like data validity / quality

dbt tests let you enforce **rules about your modeled data** (usually in the warehouse) and fail builds when rules break. Examples:

* **not_null** (required fields present)
* **unique** (no duplicate keys)
* **accepted_values** (only allowed codes/statuses)
* **relationships** (foreign key–like integrity between models)
* **custom SQL tests** (anything you can express as a query)

In practice, teams often use dbt tests as **quality gates** for curated tables (“silver/gold” models).

### Where dbt is *not* a full data quality platform

Compared to dedicated DQ/observability tools, dbt usually lacks:

* automated **profiling** and rich **rule management UI**
* built-in **anomaly detection**, drift monitoring, freshness/volume monitoring (beyond basic approaches)
* **row-level quarantine/work-queue** patterns out of the box
* end-to-end monitoring/alerting without an orchestrator or add-ons

### So what’s the best classification?

* **dbt = transformation tool with embedded data validity testing**
* It’s commonly part of a data quality strategy, but not the whole strategy.
